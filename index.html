Founder’s Story

Samskaara Vriksham was born from a mother’s journey. As a parent, I lovingly followed our traditions, rituals and shlokas — until my children began asking simple yet powerful questions: “Why do we do this?” Though I had faith, I realized I didn’t truly know how to explain the deeper meaning behind our practices.

That search for answers became a journey into Sanatana Dharma, our traditions, and the wisdom hidden within everyday rituals. At the same time, I saw many modern parents struggling — wanting to pass on values and culture, but lacking time, clarity, and guidance in today’s fast-paced digital world.

What began with one mother’s questions slowly grew into Samskaara Vriksham — a space helping children stay rooted in culture, values, mindfulness, and conscious living. Today, thousands of children and families across the world are part of this journey. 💛

A Mother’s Vision 💛

It all began with motherhood.

Though I was raised in a culturally rich family, I followed traditions with devotion. Festivals were celebrated with joy, shlokas were chanted with love, and I believed I was passing on the same values effortlessly. until my children began asking: “Why do we do this?”

But motherhood changed everything.

My sons, with their innocent yet curious minds, began asking questions— questions that stopped me in my tracks. “Why should we take a bath before breakfast?” “Why do we chant shlokas?” “Why do we follow these rituals?”

I had always followed these practices… but now, for the first time, I had to explain them.

And I didn’t have answers. That’s when the journey truly began.

With a strong will to guide my children meaningfully, I started researching, learning, and rediscovering the depth behind our traditions. What began as a mother’s search for answers slowly turned into a deeper exploration of Sanatana Dharma, our Puranas, and the science behind our practices.

Then came COVID.

With more time at home, the number of questions only grew — and not just from my children. When I began interacting with other parents, I realized something important:

👉 Many of us are following traditions… but we don’t fully understand why.

👉 Even when we know, we don’t know how to explain it to our children.

At the same time, a new challenge was emerging.

Children today are growing up in a fast-paced, digital world:

  • Constant screen exposure
  • Instant gratification
  • Endless food and entertainment choices

They are used to getting everything instantly — just like scrolling on a screen. Patience is reducing. Waiting feels difficult.

And parents?

With both parents working, stress levels rising, and support systems like grandparents often far away — there is very little time, energy, and clarity to consciously pass on values.

For families living abroad, the gap feels even wider.

That’s when the thought became clear:

“This is not just my problem.
This is the need of the hour.”

And that’s how Samskaara Vriksham was born.
